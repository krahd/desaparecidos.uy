This version keeps the original cluster/thesis architecture but removes the unnecessary conservatism in the calendar: the working assumption is high writing speed, with quality controlled by evidence, venue fit, and collision management rather than by artificially limiting the number of drafts.*

## 1. Governing principle

The objective is not simply to submit many papers. The objective is to submit **many non-overlapping, well-evidenced papers to strong venues**.

Because writing speed is high, the plan should not be conservative about drafting. It should be strict about:

- **argument distinctness**: every paper must have its own object, mechanism, argument, and readership;
- **evidence readiness**: every submission must have a concrete corpus, artwork, system, figure set, or theoretical basis adequate to its venue;
- **venue collision**: do not send two closely related papers to the same journal or to adjacent reviewer pools at the same time;
- **self-plagiarism control**: do not re-argue the same thesis under new examples;
- **source discipline**: historical, legal, and venue claims must be verified before submission.

Speed is an asset only if it is used to run **more distinct tracks**, not to make weaker slices.

## 4. Throughput engine for a very fast writer

The working model is not one draft at a time. It is **one deadline-critical paper plus multiple active non-colliding tracks**.

### Active pipeline limits

| Slot | Function | Maximum active papers | Notes |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Deadline slot | fixed external deadline | 1 | Always pre-empts all other tracks. Until 30 June: **Against Restoration**. |
| Built-artifact slots | systems/artworks already built | 3 | Best use of speed. Current candidates: 3bs, National Average, Speculative Ruins. |
| Theory/scaffolding slots | thesis-led papers from existing writing | 2 | Media Appropriation; Political-violence synthesis. |
| Opportunistic venue slot | fast CFP / special issue | 1 | Only if evidence is already ready. |
| Revision slot | returned papers | unlimited | Revisions pre-empt new drafting once review arrives. |

### Operational rules

1. **Draft aggressively across clusters.** Parallel drafting is allowed and expected.
2. **Submit conservatively within clusters.** Stagger cluster-mates unless the venues/readerships are clearly different.
3. **Never submit two papers to the same journal simultaneously.** Route one to backup or hold.
4. **Every active draft gets an evidence gate.** If the evidence is not ready, draft the scaffold but do not submit.
5. **Every draft gets a source ledger.** No legal, historical, venue, or technical claim remains unsourced at submission.

## 7. Immediate AI & SOCIETY work

### #1 Against Restoration — fixed deadline priority

The current `desaparecidos.uy` repository supports a much stronger Open Forum submission than the earlier speculative draft. It now contains a working local triptych, canonical disappeared-person corpus, reviewed target portraits, source registry, GUI/API/pipeline, crawler/traversal infrastructure, review gates, source separation, process videos, provenance sidecars, and deterministic fragment generation.

The paper should present this not as an engineering achievement but as an argument about **implemented refusal**:

> Responsible AI in memorial art may require refusing restoration, identification, photorealism, and archival substitution. In this work, refusal is implemented through coarse fragments, deterministic low-dimensional matching, review gates, source caps, provenance structures, visible incompletion, and recorded gaps.

### Exact method statements for the paper

Use these only after rechecking against code on the day of submission:

- **Fragments:** 24 × 24 px, non-overlapping by default; minimum fragment size enforced.
- **Descriptor:** six-dimensional hand-designed patch descriptor: mean RGB (3), luminance contrast (1), horizontal/vertical luminance-gradient energy (2).
- **Matching:** deterministic L2 nearest-neighbour over the descriptor matrix.
- **No learned matching:** no CLIP, no learned embeddings, no diffusion model, no semantic segmentation, no face embeddings for matching.
- **Deduplication:** perceptual hashing is used for duplicate rejection, not for matching.
- **Source contribution:** current default `max_contribution_per_source = 1`; `0` is an explicit unlimited non-default for place generation and is rejected for people generation.
- **Reuse:** per-fragment reuse is separately bounded.
- **Process videos:** show contributing approved sources / reviewed face regions and sidecar search metadata; do not claim raw rejected or non-contributing candidate images are displayed.
- **Tracked/ignored distinction:** selected target portraits and canonical person records are tracked; raw downloads, rejected candidates, crawler caches, and generated outputs remain ignored.

### Must finish before submission

1. Replace schematic figures with at least one real output/process figure.
2. Fix all method/cap/process-video/tracking statements in the manuscript.
3. Add one concrete corpus/provenance example, preferably Abeledo or another corrected record.
4. Anonymise project URL, repo paths, author-identifying prior-work blocks, and acknowledgements.
5. Confirm reference style and word count.
6. Confirm all historical/legal claims against source audit.
